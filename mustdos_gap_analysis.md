## 🔍 Notable Must-Dos Missing From the Plan

### High Priority (Iconic & Family-Friendly)

| Attraction | City | Suggested When | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **N Seoul Tower (Namsan)** | Seoul | Aug 27 afternoon — add after Gwangjang Market, before DDP | **The #1 most-mentioned Seoul landmark.** Panoramic city views, love locks, revolving restaurant. Kids love the cable car ride up and the view deck. Takes ~2 hours. |
| **Changdeokgung Palace + Huwon (Secret Garden)** | Seoul | Aug 26 morning — right after Gyeongbokgung (10-min walk) | UNESCO site, often called the most beautiful Korean palace. Secret garden tour requires advance booking but kids love exploring. Closed Wednesdays. |
| **Cheonggyecheon Stream** | Seoul | Aug 27 evening — walk it after Gwangjang Market, dinner nearby | Restored stream running through downtown. Super kid-friendly with fountains, bridges, and benches. Near Myeongdong & Gwangjang Market. |

### Medium Priority (Worth Adding If Time)

| Attraction | City | Suggested When | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Waterfalls (Cheonjeyeon or Cheonjiyeon)** | Jeju | Sep 3 — quick stop on west coast drive before/after Manjanggul | Jeju's waterfalls are short stops (20-30 min each). Kids love water. On the way from Hallim area to Seongsan. |
| **Jeju Olive Farm** | Jeju | Sep 4 — add-on near Bijarim Forest | Interactive, kid-friendly fruit farm with olive oil tasting and ice cream. Right on the coast between Bijarim and Hamdeok Beach. |
| **Incheon Chinatown / Grand Park** | Incheon | Sep 7 day trip — pick Incheon from the "to be decided" list | Great for trying Chinese-Korean fusion food, shopping at outlet mall, and Incheon Grand Park has a zoo & aquarium kids would love. |

### Lower Priority (Skipped — Good Call)

| Attraction | Why Excluded |
|---|---|
| **Everland** (theme park) | You already have Lotte World, and Everland is 1.5 hours outside Seoul — too much of a day with kids ages 4 & 6 |
| **Korea Land / Carifernia** | Water park; likely too young for the 4yo, and you have beach time in Jeju |
| **Han River Picnic** | Great experience but August is sweltering (35°C+). Your early bedtimes make river evening plans impractical |
| **Beomeosa Temple** | One of Korea's largest temples, but your Busan schedule is already jam-packed with 3 full days. Not worth squeezing in. |

## 📌 Explicitly Excluded / Already Decided In The Plan

- **"To be decided" day trip (Sep 7):** Suwon, Incheon, or Jeonju/Gangneung — not finalized yet. **Recommendation: Incheon** for a family with young kids (Incheon Grand Park has animals + aquarium + outlet shopping).
- **Hanbok rental:** Mentioned as making Gyeongbokgung free but never actually scheduled! Worth adding — kids love dressing up in traditional clothes. Book online ahead (try [Juwon Hanbok](https://juwonhanbok.com/) or [Noryangjin Hanbok Village](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noryangjin)).

## 🏆 Top 3 Recommendations To Add

1. **N Seoul Tower** on Aug 27 — fits naturally after Gwangjang Market and before DDP. It's the one thing almost every Korea itinerary includes.
2. **Changdeokgung Palace** on Aug 26 morning — it's a 10-minute walk from Gyeongbokgung, so same morning block. Two palaces in one trip.
3. **Cheonggyecheon Stream** walk on Aug 27 evening — easy add-on near your existing Myeongdong/Gwangjang area.
